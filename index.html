The Operating Model

Two operational pillars, two enabling layers — five people + Imran, twelve disciplines

Performance

AI Innovation & Enablement
Agents, automation, technology enabling both pillars
┃
Operational Pillars
Each pillar owns a product of marketing. Performance runs the revenue engine (ads, SEO, CRO, email). Brand & Creative runs the awareness engine (content, social, design). They own KPIs, output, and daily execution.
━
Enabling Layers
Strategy & Leadership (Stephen) sets direction and standards across both pillars. AI Innovation (Imran) enables both with agents, automation, and technology. They don't run operations — they make operations better.
⧖
Clean Decision Rights
Execution decisions — pillar leaders decide. Strategy and standards — enabling layers. Technology and agent design — AI Innovation. Conflicts escalate to CGO. No overlap, no ambiguity.

AI Efficiency Gains

How 5 people + Imran deliver 3-5x the output of 12 — function by function

Paid Ads3-4x
Now: 1 person managing campaigns manually
AI generates ad copy, variants, manages bids — human approves
Click to explore ↓
Before1 person writes copy, builds audiences, manages bids across Google Ads + Meta. ~10 campaigns/month.
AfterAI agents draft 3+ ad variants per campaign, auto-manage bids, build audience segments. Human sets strategy and approves. ~35 campaigns/month.
Google Ads AgentMeta AgentAd Copy AgentAudience Builder

Why it works: Ad copy generation and bid management are pattern-based tasks. AI agents produce variants at 10x speed; the human's value is strategic judgement on channel mix and ROAS targets, not copy production.

SEO5x
Now: Secondary focus, ad hoc content
AI handles keyword research, content drafts, technical audits, rank tracking
Click to explore ↓
BeforeSEO is a secondary task for the Digital Lead. ~2 optimised pieces/month. No systematic keyword tracking or technical audits.
AfterAI agents produce keyword research, draft content briefs, run technical audits, and track rankings weekly. Human sets SEO strategy and reviews output. ~10+ optimised pieces/month.
Keyword AgentContent Brief AgentTechnical Audit AgentRank Tracker

Why it works: Keyword research and content brief generation are data-intensive tasks perfectly suited to AI. The 5x comes from turning a part-time activity into a systematic, agent-driven program.

Email & Automation3x
Now: 1 dedicated person (Michael — redundant)
AI builds flows, drafts copy, tests subject lines, optimises sends
Click to explore ↓
BeforeMichael manually builds Klaviyo flows, writes email copy, sets up A/B tests. ~4 active journeys, 1 test/month.
AfterDigital & Automation Lead manages strategy; AI agents build flows, draft copy, generate subject line variants, optimise send times. ~12 active journeys, 2+ tests/month. Speed-to-lead under 1 hour.
Klaviyo Flow AgentEmail Copy AgentA/B Test AgentSend Optimiser

Why it works: Email automation is inherently AI-friendly — templates, triggers, and copy variants are systematic. The human value is journey strategy and segmentation decisions, not flow building.

CRO4x
Now: Ad hoc testing, no systematic program
AI generates landing page variants, analyses heatmaps, reports results
Click to explore ↓
BeforeCRO happens when someone has time. ~1 test every 2 months. No systematic heatmap analysis. Landing pages take days to build.
AfterAI agents generate page variants via Claude Code, analyse heatmaps, extract analytics, and report results. Digital & Automation Lead sets hypotheses and implements winners. 2+ tests/month, landing pages in <2 days.
Landing Page AgentCRO Analysis AgentHeatmap Agent

Why it works: CRO was bottlenecked by capacity, not strategy. AI removes the build constraint — generating variants is fast, so the team can test 4x more hypotheses.

Content & Copy3x
Now: 10 articles/month, 1 dedicated writer (Matthew)
AI drafts 75%+, human edits and QA. 15+ articles/month
Click to explore ↓
BeforeMatthew writes everything from scratch: blogs, newsletters, PR pitches, case studies, internal comms. ~10 articles/month, often behind schedule.
AfterAI agents draft 75%+ of content from briefs. Ops & Brand Manager sets strategy and QA, Alisha handles visual content. Newsletter, PR pitches, and blog production all agent-assisted. 15+ articles/month, zero factual errors.
Blog Draft AgentNewsletter AgentPR Pitch AgentBrand QA Agent

Why it works: AI writing agents have matured to the point where first drafts are 80% ready. The human value shifts from writing to editing, QA, and strategic direction — one person can oversee 3x the output.

Design2-3x
Now: 2 designers (lead + junior)
AI handles templates, resizing, variants. Alisha focuses on creative direction
Click to explore ↓
BeforeAlisha + Junior Designer handle all design requests. Junior does resizing, templates, variants. ~15 assets/week total. SLA pressure.
AfterAI agents handle 60%+ of production work (templates, resizing, variant generation, mockups). Alisha focuses on creative direction, campaign assets, and brand design. ~35+ assets/week. 100% SLA compliance.
Design Production AgentResize AgentVariant GeneratorMockup Agent

Why it works: 60% of design work is repetitive production (resizing for platforms, template variants, format conversions). AI handles this at speed, freeing the designer for work that requires genuine creative judgement.

Social Media2-3x
Now: 5 posts/week, 1 dedicated person (Miranda)
AI drafts posts, schedules, monitors ORM. 7+ posts/week
Click to explore ↓
BeforeMiranda writes all social copy, schedules posts, monitors reviews, responds to comments. 5 posts/week, 20 reviews/month. ORM response times variable.
AfterAI agents draft 70%+ of posts, auto-schedule across platforms, monitor ORM 24/7, and draft review responses for human approval. Miranda manages calendar and creative direction. 7+ posts/week, 30+ reviews/month, same-day response guaranteed.
Social Scheduling AgentORM Response AgentContent Draft AgentEngagement Monitor

Why it works: Social content creation and scheduling is highly templatable. AI agents handle the volume; the human provides brand voice, community judgement, and escalation management for sensitive reviews.

Marketing OpsNear-full automation
Now: 2 coordinators + manual Asana, reporting, scheduling
AI handles routing, SLA tracking, reporting, meeting notes
Click to explore ↓
BeforeMarilyn and Kayla manually manage Asana tasks, schedule meetings, take notes, track SLAs, route requests, compile reports. 2 full-time coordination roles.
AfterAI agents auto-log briefs from email/Slack, route requests in Asana, track SLAs with automated escalation, generate meeting notes, and compile dashboards. Ops & Brand Manager focuses on strategic coordination and stakeholder management. Zero coordination headcount.
Asana Automation AgentSLA Tracking AgentReport Generation AgentBrief Intake AgentMeeting Notes Agent

Why it works: Coordination is the most automatable marketing function. Task routing, SLA monitoring, and report generation are rule-based processes that AI agents handle with higher consistency than manual tracking.
