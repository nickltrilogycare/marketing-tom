Why Change

The Bottleneck Isn't Talent

The bottleneck in a traditional marketing team isn't talent — it's handoffs. A campaign touches 4-5 people before it ships. Each handoff adds latency, context loss, and rework.

Today, Trilogy Care's marketing function has 12 people covering 12 disciplines. They are organised in specialist silos: the writer writes, the designer designs, the social person posts, the ads person runs ads. Each person is a single point of failure for their discipline.

No one person can take a brief and ship a finished piece of work. Every campaign passes through 4-5 hands before it reaches a customer. And every handoff creates latency, context loss, and rework. There is no AI-native capability, no CRO practice, no competitor intelligence function, and limited web development capacity.

The team is structured for a world where execution is the bottleneck. But AI has changed what the bottleneck is. When AI can draft copy, generate design variants, build email flows, and schedule social posts — the scarce resource is no longer execution capacity. It's judgment.

12 people. 12 disciplines. 4-5 handoffs per campaign. Two critical capability gaps.
The Builder Model

From Specialists to Builders

When AI handles 70% of execution, the scarce resource isn't skill — it's judgment. Knowing what's worth building, what good looks like, and when to ship.

The builder model borrows from developer culture. A builder is a pi-shaped operator — deep in one or two areas, competent across the full pod scope, and comfortable directing AI agents to fill the gaps. Builders take a brief and deliver finished output. No handoffs. No waiting.

AI handles the volume: first drafts, design variants, data analysis, scheduling, monitoring. The builder handles the 30% that requires human judgment: strategy, quality assurance, brand voice, stakeholder relationships, and the decision of when something is good enough to ship.

After

7 builders in two pods. Brief in, output out. Judgment bottleneck (not capacity). Builders cross-cover. 30-40 AI agents handling execution. Gaps closed.

What Needs to Go Right

The Bets We're Making

The builder model is a bet. AI + good-enough judgment > deep specialist + slow handoffs. Here's what needs to go right for that bet to pay off.

Unicorn Hire Risk

Finding a Performance Builder who is genuinely multi-skilled across 7 disciplines AND AI-native is hard. May need 6-8 weeks and a premium salary. Paul covers execution gaps during the hiring period. If the hire doesn't work, the performance pod is understaffed for months.

Bus Factor

One Performance Builder = single point of failure for the revenue engine. Mitigation: Paul can cover, AI agents retain workflows. Consider a second builder long-term if the pod grows. But for now, this is a calculated risk.

Builder vs Specialist Labels

If Matthew only writes, Miranda only does social, and Alisha only designs — they are specialists with builder labels. The 90-day assessment framework addresses this directly: cross-training must be demonstrated, not just planned.

Builder Burnout

Research shows 60% burnout in IT generalists. Broad scope + AI management is heavy cognitive load. Monitor actively, protect capacity. AI must reduce load, not just increase output expectations. If builders are drowning, the model isn't working.

Quality Depth Trade-off

Builders won't match specialists in any single discipline. The bet: AI + good-enough judgment > deep specialist + slow handoffs. May not hold for complex technical SEO or advanced attribution. Watch for quality dips in specialist-depth areas.

Marketing Ops Manager Is Critical Path

The internal agency pod has three builders who need coaching through the transition from specialist to generalist. Without a strong Ops Manager, the cross-training stalls, builder enablement fails, and you have three specialists with new job titles. This hire matters as much as the Performance Builder.

This isn't about doing more with less. It's about building differently — with people who can flex, AI that can execute, and a structure that lets both do their best work.
