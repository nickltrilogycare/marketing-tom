The Constraints

Every marketing team hits the same bottlenecks. These are the five structural constraints that slow teams down — and the data that proves it.

01
Campaign Launch — 3-4 weeks typical → target <1 week

41% of teams take 3-4 weeks to launch a campaign. Only 3.6% go live in under a week.

Root Causes

  • 37%Revision loops between creative and stakeholders
  • 35%Feedback collection delays across departments
  • 34%Content creation bottleneck (copy + design sequential)
  • 29%Cross-functional requests requiring multiple team inputs

Constraint: Every campaign touches briefing → copy → design → QA → approval → launch. Each handoff adds 1-3 days.

02
Content Production — 8-12 hours/article → target 1.5-2.5 hours

Average blog post: 3h48m writing alone. Full workflow including research, SEO, editing, and approvals: 8-12 hours.

Root Causes

  • •Research time consuming 30-40% of total production
  • •Blank-page problem — starting from zero every time
  • •SEO treated as post-production step instead of input
  • •Multiple editing cycles before publish

Constraint: Sequential process (research → write → edit → SEO → approve) instead of parallel.

03
Design Bottleneck — 70% of designers do work below their skill level

75% of creative leaders report insufficient capacity. 76% experienced burnout. 55% of projects labelled "high priority" — meaning nothing is actually prioritised.

Root Causes

  • •Resizing and reformatting consuming skilled designer hours
  • •Brief interpretation — unclear briefs requiring back-and-forth
  • •Revision cycles driven by subjective feedback
  • •Priority chaos — everything marked urgent, nothing triaged
04
Approval & Governance — 53% say process too burdensome

37% flag revisions/approvals as the major bottleneck. Cycles extend 40% longer when everything routes through leadership.

Root Causes

  • •Unclear decision rights — nobody knows who actually approves
  • •Too many "consulted" stakeholders in every workflow
  • •No tiered governance — social posts get the same review as brand campaigns

Key data point: Organisations with explicit decision rights enjoy 23% greater revenue growth.

05
Priority Chaos — 55% of projects labelled "high priority"

38% of marketers feel stretched or overloaded. 77% report increased project volume year-over-year with no corresponding increase in capacity.

Root Causes

  • •No intake system — requests arrive via email, Slack, hallway conversations
  • •No scoring framework — decisions made by who shouts loudest
  • •No trade-off conversations — "yes" without asking "instead of what?"
  • •Reactive culture that rewards firefighting over planning
"The constraints aren't about people. They're structural. Fix the structure — with AI, governance, and rhythm — and the same team produces 3x the output."

Prioritisation Framework

Every request gets scored. No exceptions. The ICE framework gives the team a shared language for trade-off conversations.

ICE Score = (Impact + Confidence + Ease) / 3

DimensionScaleWhat It Measures
Impact1-10How much will this move a KPI?
Confidence1-10How sure are we this will work? (Data-backed = high, gut feel = low)
Ease1-10How quickly can we ship this? (Hours = 10, weeks = 2)

Score ≥7: ship this sprint. Score 5-7: next sprint. Score <5: backlog or decline.

Work Intake Rules

  • 1Single channel: All requests enter through one channel (Asana form → AI brief intake agent). No Slack requests. No hallway asks.
  • 2Required fields: Goal alignment, deadline, expected impact, effort estimate. Incomplete briefs returned automatically.
  • 3ICE-scored: Every request ICE-scored before entering the sprint. No score = no entry.
  • 4Visible backlog: All requests displayed with status — nothing hidden, nothing forgotten.
  • 5Trade-off conversations: "Yes, but which of these three things should we stop?" Every new "yes" requires a "no."
  • 6RACI per request type: Who Decides, who's Consulted, who's Informed — defined once, enforced always.

Tiered Governance

Not everything needs the same level of review. Speed-forward what you can. Gate what you must.

Low Risk — Auto-approve

Scope: Social posts using approved templates, templated design, routine emails

Process: Auto-approve. Human-on-the-loop monitoring (spot-check 10-20%). No approval gate.

Medium Risk — Peer Review

Scope: Blog articles, campaign copy, new design concepts

Process: Peer review + Marketing Ops Manager sign-off. 24-hour SLA.

High Risk — Full Review

Scope: Brand campaigns, PR, regulated content, new channel launches

Process: Full review: peer + Ops Manager + Stephen. 48-hour SLA.

"Governance should accelerate 80% of work and protect 20%. If it slows everything equally, it's not governance — it's bureaucracy."

Quality Gates

Three-layer QA system. Automated catches the obvious. Peer review catches the nuanced. Brand governance catches the strategic.

Layer 1 — Automated (AI)

Machine QA

What it catches: Spelling, grammar, plagiarism, brand voice drift, formatting, link validation, rendering issues

Tools: Grammarly, AI brand voice checker, Copyscape

Layer 2 — Peer Review (Human)

Builder QA

What it catches: Fact accuracy, readability, brief alignment, tone consistency, audience fit

Method: Read aloud, cross-reference claims, run checklist. Every piece of content reviewed by one other builder before publish.

Layer 3 — Brand Governance

Strategic QA

What it catches: Brand compliance, claims substantiation, accessibility, legal/regulatory alignment

Method: Monthly brand audit (AI-assisted), style guide enforcement, quarterly competitive review

"Quality at speed means building quality into the process — not bolting it on at the end. Automated QA catches 80% of errors in seconds. Humans catch the 20% that matters."
