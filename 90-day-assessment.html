90-Day Builder Trial

A structured assessment to determine whether Matthew, Miranda, and Alisha can transition from specialists to builders — or whether we hire externally.

"The question isn't whether they're good at their current job. It's whether they can become something different — a builder who ships across disciplines with AI doing the heavy lifting."

Assessment Principles

What we're testing

The builder model requires three capabilities that specialists typically don't have. The 90-day trial tests all three:

CapabilityWhat It MeansHow We Measure
AI-Native ExecutionUses AI agents for 70%+ of production. Doesn't just "try AI" — it's their default workflow.AI-drafted output ratio, agent usage logs, time savings
Cross-Discipline FlexProduces work outside their core discipline. A writer creating social assets. A social manager producing design. Not just "helping" — shipping.Output count in non-core disciplines, quality at builder standard
Self-Directed ShippingTakes a brief and delivers finished output without hand-holding. Doesn't wait for approval on every decision. Ships, then iterates.Brief-to-delivery cycle time, manager intervention frequency

What we're NOT testing

  • ✕Whether they're good at their current specialist role (we already know)
  • ✕Whether they "like" AI tools (irrelevant — it's a capability requirement)
  • ✕Attitude or effort (we're measuring output and capability, not intent)
  • ✕Whether they can learn in theory (we're measuring whether they perform in practice within 90 days)

The 90 Days

Days 1–30

Foundation — Learn the Builder Workflow

Each person receives their AI agent toolkit (built by Imran), a cross-training plan, and a clear brief on what "builder standard" looks like. They start producing in their core discipline using AI agents for the majority of execution.

Day 30 Checkpoint

MeasureTarget
AI-drafted output ratio≥40% of total output AI-assisted
AI agents in daily use≥2 agents used daily
Core discipline output maintainedNo volume or quality drop from current baseline
Cross-training sessions attended100% — no opt-outs

Pass criteria: Demonstrating willingness and early capability. Not yet at builder standard — that's Day 60.

Red flag: AI agent usage below 20%, resistance to cross-training, or output quality declining.

Days 31–60

Stretch — Cross-Discipline Production

The real test begins. Each person starts producing outside their core discipline — not "helping" or "supporting" but actually shipping finished work in adjacent areas. AI agents handle the skill gap; the person handles judgment and QA.

Day 60 Checkpoint

MeasureTarget
AI-drafted output ratio≥60% of total output AI-assisted
Cross-discipline output≥2 pieces/week shipped outside core discipline
Quality in non-core work≥80% first-pass quality (judged by Marketing Ops Manager)
Brief-to-delivery cycle timeImproving vs Day 30 baseline
Pod cross-coverageCan cover for one other builder for 2+ days without quality drop

Pass criteria: Producing real, shippable work outside their comfort zone. Not perfect — but builder-standard (good enough to ship, not specialist-standard).

Red flag: Cross-discipline output below 1 piece/week, quality in non-core work below 60%, or still relying on other builders to finish their work.

Days 61–90

Prove — Operate as a Builder

The final 30 days. Each person operates as a full builder — taking briefs across their pod's disciplines, directing AI agents for 70% of execution, shipping end-to-end without manager intervention. This is the standard they need to sustain.

Day 90 Final Assessment

MeasureTargetWeight
AI-drafted output ratio≥70%25%
Cross-discipline output volume≥3 pieces/week shipped outside core25%
Quality across all disciplines≥85% first-pass quality20%
Brief-to-delivery speed≤2 business days for standard briefs15%
Pod cross-coverage demonstratedSuccessfully covered another builder's full workload for 1 week15%

Scoring: Weighted average across all 5 measures. Each measure scored 0-100.

Outcomes

The Day 90 assessment produces a weighted score. Three possible outcomes:

≥75 — Pass: Confirmed as Builder

Person transitions permanently into the builder role. KPIs updated to builder scorecard. Cross-training continues but person is confirmed.

50–74 — Extend: 30-Day Extension

Showing progress but not yet at builder standard. One 30-day extension with specific targets. If still below 75 at Day 120 → Exit.

<50 — Exit: Hire Externally

Not able to transition to builder model within the required timeframe. Managed exit with support. External hire for the builder role begins immediately.

Assessment Evidence

Each checkpoint requires documented evidence, not opinion:

  • 1AI usage data — agent interaction logs, AI-drafted vs human-drafted ratio (from Imran's tracking)
  • 2Output log — every piece produced, tagged by discipline (core vs cross-discipline)
  • 3Quality audit — random sample of 10 pieces, scored for first-pass quality by Marketing Ops Manager
  • 4Cycle time data — average brief-to-delivery time from Asana
  • 5Cross-coverage test — did they successfully cover another builder's work for 2+ days?
  • 6Stakeholder feedback — did internal clients notice a quality change? (CSAT data)

If We Hire Externally

"The 90-day trial is honest, not hopeful. If the current person can't become a builder, we hire someone who already is one."

External Builder Profile

If any of the three roles go to external hire, we're looking for:

  • →Content & Comms Builder: 3-5 years agency content marketing. Multi-format (blogs, social, PR, comms). Already using AI for 50%+ output. Can write AND brief design. Self-directed.
  • →Social, ORM & Design Builder: 3-5 years as a "social media creative" or content creator. Shoots, edits, designs, writes, posts. Strong with AI design and video tools. ORM experience. Agency or multi-brand background.
  • →Creative & Campaign Builder: 3-5 years agency graphic designer. Produces across print, digital, social, video. Already using AI design tools for 50%+ production. Can write campaign messaging. Fast, volume-oriented.

Hiring timeline: 4-6 weeks from decision to start date. The Marketing Ops Manager leads the search with Stephen's sign-off. Imran provides AI proficiency assessment during the interview process.
